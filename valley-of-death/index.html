Valley of Death

The First Valley

Small contracts expire. The major program of record hasn’t materialized. The gap between short-term pilots and long-term production deals is where most defense startups stall out or die.

~90% of defense startups never reach production

Peak 2

The Award

The program of record lands. Multi-year, high-volume production. The company celebrated. But the contract comes with expectations: deliver at scale, on time, at cost. And that’s where the real problem starts.

$933B in DoD contracts awarded FY2023

The Drop

Who Builds It?

Scale demands a workforce that doesn’t exist. 250,000 workers needed for the Navy alone. 3.8 million for manufacturing. The average defense worker is 55. The pipeline is empty.

Avg. age of skilled defense worker: 55

2nd Valley of Death

The Second Valley

Defense startups won the contracts. They proved the technology. But they can’t build at scale because the hands and the expertise simply aren’t there. This is the valley of death that will define the next decade.

3.8M manufacturing jobs unfilled by 2033

We’ll need both people and robots to bring the US out of that second valley. Nidus is building the robots.
