Careers at Nidus

About Nidus

Today, we design industrial equipment for human operators. As fewer Americans choose to work in manufacturing, we need to automate factory operations.

Nidus builds adaptive, decision-making robots that operate the high-precision, rule-based machines that have powered factories for decades. We’re starting with the defense industrial base, the largest buyer of US-made parts, with the goal of reducing costs enough to compete in the commercial market.

The Role

We are seeking a Staff Hardware Engineer to help build the future of manufacturing automation. You will be driving the integration of our robots with industrial manufacturing equipment from the mechanical and electrical side. This role is for someone who is scrappy and willing to move quickly and iterate rapidly while keeping long-term design goals in mind.

Salary   $200,000 – $275,000
Equity   Meaningful equity stake in an early-stage company

Key Responsibilities

  • Lead integration efforts between robotic arms, grippers, sensors, and manufacturing equipment.
  • Own the entire assembly, including electronics, sensors, and mechanical components.
  • Support deployment of systems to customer facilities and provide technical guidance during installation and commissioning.
  • Stay current with emerging technologies in robotics, additive manufacturing, and automated manufacturing systems.

Required Qualifications

  • Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering or related field.
  • 8+ years of experience in mechanical design and development, preferably in robotics or manufacturing automation.
  • Strong proficiency in CAD software (SolidWorks, Fusion 360, or similar) and stress/strain analysis.
  • Experience with mechanical design for robotic systems, including kinematics, dynamics, and structural analysis.
  • Knowledge of manufacturing processes including machining, 3D printing, and assembly techniques.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Experience with polymers and carbon fiber material properties and manufacturing processes.
  • Experience with force-controlled robotic systems, collaborative robots (cobots), and dual-arm robotic systems.
  • Knowledge of sensor integration and mechatronics.

Location

This position is in-person in New York City. We are not hiring remotely at this time.
