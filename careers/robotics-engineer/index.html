Careers at Nidus

About Nidus

Today, we design industrial equipment for human operators. As fewer Americans choose to work in manufacturing, we need to automate factory operations.

Nidus builds adaptive, decision-making robots that operate the high-precision, rule-based machines that have powered factories for decades. We’re starting with the defense industrial base, the largest buyer of US-made parts, with the goal of reducing costs enough to compete in the commercial market.

The Role

We are seeking a Staff Robotics Engineer to bring next-generation robotics algorithms and Vision-Language-Action models to production manufacturing environments. This role is for someone who is scrappy and willing to move quickly and iterate rapidly while keeping long-term design goals in mind.

Salary   $200,000 – $275,000
Equity   Meaningful equity stake in an early-stage company

Key Responsibilities

  • Design, develop, and deploy robotic systems integrating hardware components with sophisticated software control algorithms.
  • Lead technical architecture decisions for robotics platforms, including mechanical design, electronics integration, and software frameworks.
  • Support sensor fusion and latency improvement efforts.
  • Own the sim-to-real pipeline of our robotics algorithms.
  • Develop and implement control systems, inverse kinematics algorithms, and safety algorithms.
  • Prototype and test robotic systems in real-world environments, iterating based on performance data.

Required Qualifications

  • 7+ years of experience in robotics development, with demonstrated expertise in both hardware and software integration.
  • Strong proficiency in robotics frameworks (ROS/ROS2) and programming languages (C++, Python).
  • Experience with sensor fusion, computer vision, SLAM, or motion planning algorithms.
  • Hands-on experience with manufacturing processes and production-grade hardware development.
  • Proven track record of shipping robotic products or systems from concept to deployment.

Preferred Qualifications

  • Familiarity with embedded systems, real-time operating systems, and edge computing.
  • Familiarity with GPU performance optimization.
  • Experience with AI/ML applications in robotics, including deep learning for perception and control.
  • Strong understanding of mechanical design, CAD tools, and design for manufacturing principles.

Location

This position is in-person in New York City. We are not hiring remotely at this time.
